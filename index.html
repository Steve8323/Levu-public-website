What We Build

A Neural Network That Runs on Light

Levu's photonic integrated circuits perform matrix multiplication, nonlinear activation, and layer-to-layer propagation entirely in the optical domain. The result: neural network inference at the speed of light, with power consumption orders of magnitude below conventional silicon.

01
Optical Matrix Multiply

Coherent interference in a mesh of Mach-Zehnder interferometers computes any linear transformation in a single pass — no multiply-accumulate units needed.

02
Nonlinear Activation

On-chip nonlinear optical elements apply activation functions directly in the photonic domain, eliminating electro-optic conversion between layers.

03
WDM Layer Stacking

Wavelength-division multiplexing runs multiple neural network layers on the same physical waveguide simultaneously — depth at zero additional cost.

Why Levu

Light Does What Silicon Cannot

Every conventional AI chip is bottlenecked by the same physics: electrons moving through resistive material generate heat, consume power, and max out bandwidth. Scaling means more chips, more cooling, more energy.

Photons carry no charge. They don't generate heat when they compute. A single waveguide carries hundreds of independent wavelength channels simultaneously. Levu's photonic neural network chips exploit all of this natively.

Our nonlinear PIC platform is the first to bring true end-to-end optical inference — input to output, no electrical intermediaries — to a manufacturable silicon photonics process.

FAQ

Common Questions

Light passes through a mesh of tunable beam splitters (Mach-Zehnder interferometers). The interference pattern of the light performs matrix multiplication — the core operation of every neural network layer — in a single optical pass at the speed of light.
Most optical accelerators still convert between optical and electronic domains between layers. Levu's nonlinear PIC platform performs activation functions optically, enabling true end-to-end photonic inference for the first time.
Our platform maps naturally onto fully connected layers, CNNs via optical convolution, and transformers via attention head parallelism across WDM channels. We provide a compiler that converts standard PyTorch models to photonic weight configurations.
Our first multi-layer inference chip is in tape-out at a silicon photonics foundry. Early access partner programs are open — contact us to learn more.
