Core Technology

Nonlinear Optics on a Chip

A computer that can only perform linear operations is not very interesting. Every general-purpose processor needs nonlinear operations — notoriously hard in optics. Our technology is built around generating these nonlinearities in a photonic integrated circuit (PIC), enabling true all-optical cross-domain computation.

01
Nonlinear PIC Components

Our integrated nonlinear optical components enable optical logic gates in the digital domain, complex mathematical operations in the analog domain, and all-optical feed-forward for continuous variables in the quantum domain.

02
Optical Memory

True in-chip optical memory — a critical missing piece for general-purpose all-optical processors. Our nonlinear building blocks make it possible without any conversion back to electronics.

03
All-Optical ADC

By combining our optical logic and analog math capabilities, we built the first all-optical analog-digital converter — bridging compute domains on a single photonic chip.
